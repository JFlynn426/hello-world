The Joys of Nature Photography

Sparking my interest

In 2009 I traveled to Costa Rica with my wife with an old hand me down camera from my father. I loved seeing amazing wildlife in Costa Rica and the endless variation of thousands of different species residing in the Costa Rican Rainforest. While the pictures I took were frequently blurry or out of focus I discovered how much I enjoyed trying to take pictures and bring home photos of this magical place.

Finding my passion

After my trip to Costa Rica I began brining my camera along when I would go out and explore. I started spending my time out in the Connecticut woods and reading in my free time about the ecology and natural variation in the state that I lived. It became important for me to find the most obscure species that I could culminating in my search for a critically endangered species in my state the Timber Rattlesnake. The Timber Rattlesnake had nearly been extirpated from Connecticut by homeowners killing the snakes when they entered their property and by people capturing them for the pet trade. It took nearly the entire summer but eventually I was rewarded with finding them in a small area in the largest park in the state.

Mastering my passion

After thousands and thousands of hours, investment in better photography gear my pictures improved immensely. I have been published in magazines and sold thousands of dollars worth of photos.
