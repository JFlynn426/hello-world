Nature Photography by John Flynn

John Flynn

I have always loved technology ever since my father brought home one of the first Apple Computers. My passion for technology blossomed and I started building my own computers in about 2003. I have always loved learning how things work and the challenge of building computers and working with different applications facinated me. Shortly after Apple switched to Intel processors I started building hackintoshes. It was fun to work with the combination of hardware and basic code to get a self assembled computer to run the Mac Operating System.

I started school late after and began working as a laboratory technician at UConn where I was going to school. After years invested in going to school and working in various labs I realized that while I enjoy the academics and experiment design that it would require a lot of schooling to get a Ph.D.. With less and less money being devoted to biological research it became clear that finishing a college degree and a Ph.D. was going to take a long time for an uncertain future. I decided that I was going to try following a passion of mine: nature photography. While I enjoyed it very much, I learned that it would be difficult for me to make enough money to live the life that I wanted to live. I am not the best at marketing my material and myself and it is the primary component of being a successful nature photographer.

I decided to look at one of my earlier passions, computers for a more practical way of supporting myself and my wife. I learned that I love coding. It allows me to exercize my love for logical problem solving on a daily basis.

Following your passion
